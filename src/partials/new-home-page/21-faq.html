Best Practices in Risk Management

Set a Budget: Before starting to play, determine a fixed amount you’re willing to spend. Only use money that you can afford to lose without affecting your essential expenses.

Set Limits: Operators offer responsible gambling tools that allow patrons to set daily or weekly spending limits or restrict the amount of time spent playing. This helps control your gaming activity.

Know Your Time to Quit: Set win/loss limits in their clear amount. For example, you can determine in advance when to cash out on a certain amount in profit or exactly how much is lost.

Avoid Chasing Losses: Understand that losing is a natural part of the game. Trying to recover losses by increasing bets can lead to greater financial risks.

Session Time Tracking: Keeps count of the time spent playing. Deposit and Wagering Limit: Putting a limit on a certain amount that can be deposited or wagered within a given time. Self-Exclusion Options: The ability for players to take a temporary or permanent break from gaming.
